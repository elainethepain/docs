## Overview

Strategy metrics are essential for understanding the performance and health of Kamino's various products. These metrics help users make informed decisions about their investments and risk management.

## Key Metrics Explained

### Total Value Locked (TVL)

TVL represents the total value of assets locked in Kamino's protocols.

**What it tells you:**
- **Protocol Size**: Overall scale and adoption
- **Market Confidence**: User trust in the protocol
- **Liquidity Depth**: Available liquidity for transactions
- **Growth Trends**: Protocol expansion over time

### Annual Percentage Yield (APY)

APY represents the annualized return on investment, including compound interest.

**Types of APY:**
- **Supply APY**: Interest earned on supplied assets
- **Borrow APY**: Interest paid on borrowed assets
- **Net APY**: Difference between earning and borrowing rates
- **Strategy APY**: Returns from automated strategies

### Annual Percentage Rate (APR)

APR represents the simple annual interest rate without compounding.

**When to use APR vs APY:**
- **APR**: For simple interest calculations
- **APY**: For compound interest scenarios
- **Comparison**: APY is typically higher than APR due to compounding

## Performance Metrics

### Utilization Rate

The percentage of supplied assets that are currently borrowed.

**Impact on Rates:**
- **Low Utilization**: Lower borrow rates, higher supply rates
- **High Utilization**: Higher borrow rates, lower supply rates
- **Optimal Range**: 60-80% typically provides balanced rates

### Health Factor

A measure of position safety and liquidation risk.

**Health Factor Ranges:**
- **> 2.0**: Very safe, minimal risk
- **1.5 - 2.0**: Safe, good buffer
- **1.0 - 1.5**: Caution zone
- **< 1.0**: At risk of liquidation

### Net APY

The difference between what you earn and what you pay.

**Calculation:**
```
Net APY = Supply APY - Borrow APY
```

**Interpretation:**
- **Positive Net APY**: Profitable position
- **Negative Net APY**: Unprofitable position
- **Zero Net APY**: Break-even point

## Strategy-Specific Metrics

### Multiply Metrics

#### Leverage Ratio
- **Current Leverage**: Your position's current leverage
- **Max Leverage**: Maximum allowed leverage
- **Optimal Leverage**: Best leverage for your risk tolerance

#### Liquidation Distance
- **Current Distance**: How far from liquidation
- **Buffer Zone**: Safe distance from liquidation threshold
- **Risk Level**: Low, medium, or high risk

### Liquidity Vault Metrics

#### Impermanent Loss
- **Current IL**: Current impermanent loss
- **IL Protection**: Available protection mechanisms
- **IL Recovery**: Strategies to recover from IL

#### Pool Performance
- **Pool APY**: Overall pool returns
- **Volume**: Trading volume in the pool
- **Fees Earned**: Trading fees collected

## Best Practices

### Monitoring Metrics
1. **Daily Checks**: Monitor key metrics regularly
2. **Trend Analysis**: Look for patterns over time
3. **Comparative Analysis**: Compare with market benchmarks
4. **Risk Assessment**: Use metrics to assess risk

### Decision Making
1. **APY vs Risk**: Balance returns with risk tolerance
2. **Utilization Impact**: Understand how utilization affects rates
3. **Health Factor**: Keep health factor above 1.5
4. **Diversification**: Don't over-concentrate in single strategies

## Advanced Metrics

### Sharpe Ratio
- **Risk-Adjusted Returns**: Returns relative to risk
- **Volatility**: Price fluctuation measures
- **Optimal Ratios**: Target ratios for different strategies

### Maximum Drawdown
- **Historical Losses**: Worst historical losses
- **Recovery Time**: Time to recover from losses
- **Risk Tolerance**: Align with your risk tolerance

### Correlation Analysis
- **Asset Correlations**: How assets move together
- **Diversification Benefits**: Reducing portfolio risk
- **Strategy Selection**: Choosing complementary strategies
